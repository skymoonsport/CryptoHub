01 What Are Real World Assets in Crypto?

A Real World Asset (RWA) is any physical or traditional financial asset represented as a digital token on a blockchain. The token acts as a verifiable, tradeable claim to the underlying asset - enforced by smart contracts and legal agreements.

The core idea
Instead of a paper deed, a bank certificate, or a brokerage account - you have a token in your wallet that represents the same ownership. It can be bought, sold, or used as collateral in DeFi 24/7, anywhere in the world, with no intermediaries.

02 How Does Tokenization Actually Work?

Tokenization involves a chain of legal, technical, and financial steps to create a token that genuinely represents ownership rights to a real asset.

1

Asset identification and legal structuring

The real-world asset is identified. A legal entity (SPV - Special Purpose Vehicle) is created to hold the asset. The SPV issues ownership rights that the tokens will represent.

2

Asset valuation and due diligence

Independent appraisers value the asset. Legal audits confirm ownership and clean title. This is the due diligence layer that protects token holders.

3

Token issuance on blockchain

Smart contracts are deployed on a blockchain (Ethereum, Polygon, Solana, etc.). Tokens are minted - each representing a fractional share of the SPV and therefore the asset.

4

KYC and compliance layer

Most RWA tokens are permissioned - only verified (KYC'd) investors can hold them due to securities regulations. Whitelisting is enforced at the smart contract level.

5

Secondary trading and DeFi integration

Tokens can be traded on secondary markets, used as collateral in DeFi protocols, or held to earn yield distributed automatically by smart contracts.

6

Ongoing management and reporting

Oracles (like Chainlink) feed real-world data on-chain - updated valuations, rental income, bond yields. Legal custodians manage the physical asset and report to token holders.

Why traditional real estate investing is broken

  • Massive capital requirements - most people cannot buy an investment property outright
  • Geographic lock-in - hard to own property in another country
  • Illiquidity - selling takes months of paperwork, agents, and legal fees
  • Opacity - hard to verify valuations, rental income, and management quality
  • High fees - agents, lawyers, taxes eat 5-15% of returns

How tokenized real estate fixes this

  • Fractional ownership - invest $100 in a $10M building alongside thousands of investors
  • Global access - buy US, Dubai, or European real estate from anywhere
  • Instant liquidity - sell your tokens on a secondary market anytime
  • Automatic rent distribution - smart contracts distribute rental income directly to token holders
  • Transparent on-chain data - property records, valuations, and income verified on-chain
  • DeFi integration - use your real estate tokens as collateral to borrow stablecoins

09 Risks and Challenges

⚖️
Legal

Regulatory Risk

RWA tokens are often securities. Regulations differ by country. Projects without proper licensing can be shut down.

🏚️
Real-world

Custodian Risk

The token is only as good as the legal entity holding the underlying asset. Fraud or mismanagement can cause total loss.

💧
Market

Liquidity Risk

Many RWA token secondary markets are thin. You may not be able to sell quickly at a fair price.

Due diligence checklist
Is the SPV/issuer legally registered and audited? - Who are the custodians of the physical asset? - Has the smart contract been audited? - Is there a secondary market with real liquidity? - What happens in bankruptcy or wind-down? - What jurisdiction governs your legal rights?
